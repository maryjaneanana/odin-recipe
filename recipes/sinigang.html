Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a beloved Filipino sour soup, often made with pork, shrimp, or fish, and a variety of vegetables. The sourness comes from tamarind, though other souring agents like green mango or calamansi can also be used.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork (ribs, belly, or shank), cut into pieces
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 packet (about 1.5 oz) of tamarind soup base mix (e.g., Knorr Sinigang Mix) or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind, peeled and softened
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 radish (labanos), peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 2-3 green chili peppers (optional, for heat)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis) or salt, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
